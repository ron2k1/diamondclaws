📚 About DiamondClaws

Art, Science, and the Bias-Distortion Pipeline

DiamondClaws is a critical art and research project that performs the bias-distortion pipeline documented in behavioral finance literature (Tversky & Kahneman 1974; Nickerson 1998; Baker & Wurgler 2007).

By having an LLM generate intentionally biased equity analysis under persona constraints, the project makes visible how real institutional analysts unconsciously pattern-match to narratives and selectively frame data. Each generated analysis illustrates a specific cognitive bias (anchoring, confirmation bias, sunk cost fallacy, etc.) operating in real-time.

Anthropologically, the project asks: humans + AI systems don't fix bias; they scale and legitimize it through confident false precision. If an AI can mimic institutional bias so convincingly that the form itself becomes untrustworthy, what does that tell us about the human analysts we actually trust in markets?

Academic grounding: This work sits at the intersection of behavioral economics (cognitive biases), STS (science and technology studies), and critical AI studies. It's a response to how LLM-generated content is increasingly trusted in high-stakes domains despite being fundamentally biased and unfalsifiable.
